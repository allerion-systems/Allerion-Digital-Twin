Executive Summary

The owner retains 100% of the mineral estate and digital-twin IP. All physical exploration, drilling, and extraction are pushed to third parties under lease, joint-venture, or service contract. The operator personally executes only the high-leverage activities: title custody, data licensing, grant capture, and counterparty negotiation.

This model is engineered for asymmetric upside on minimal capital exposure. The largest single check the operator writes in Year 1 is the ISGS core re-analysis (≤$5K, identified in USBM RI 4315 as the lowest-cost path to value inflection).

Strategic Thesis

The same property that hosts the critical-mineral asset can host the compute that processes it. Three structural advantages collapse into one site: (1) physical security of a private 40-acre parcel; (2) thermal access to Mississippian limestone bedrock for year-round 12–14 °C ground-source cooling; (3) commodity hedge — fluorspar tonnage extracted on-property funds the build, and downstream fluoropolymer chemistry (CaF₂ → HF → fluoropolymers) is the same supply chain that produces immersion-cooling dielectric fluids and semiconductor etchants.

The compute node is positioned as a Tier-I edge facility for federal mineral-data workloads: USGS Earth MRI hosting, DoD strategic-materials AI inference, NURE/ISGS data-lake mirroring, and digital-twin rendering for procurement reviews. The customer base does not require hyperscale; it requires jurisdictional control and physical custody.

The Material→Compute Linkage

  • CaF₂ → HF acid: on-property fluorspar is the upstream feedstock for hydrofluoric acid, the primary etchant for every silicon wafer in every server in every data center.
  • HF → fluoropolymers: PTFE / FEP / PFA jackets coat the high-frequency cables, gaskets, and immersion-cooling dielectrics inside the same facility.
  • Limestone host rock: the same Mississippian carbonates that contain the fluorite veins also serve as a high-thermal-mass cooling sink at 80–300 ft depth.
  • REE (Nd, Dy, Pr): if confirmed on-site, these are the magnets in every server fan, drive spindle, and grid-tie inverter the facility runs on.
  • Tailings revenue: 200–500 T pilot fluorspar extraction at metallurgical grade ($350–450/T) directly capitalizes site preparation.

The "Build It From The Hill" Principle

The user-stated invariant for this study is simple: if there is fluorspar, the facility gets built — at minimum — from the materials on the property. That principle is interpreted at two tiers:

Financial tier: a modest pilot extraction (200–500 T metallurgical-grade fluorspar) self-finances the foundation and shell. At $350–450/T this is $70–225K — sufficient to cover concrete, fuel, and site prep for a 10,000 ft² building.

Physical tier: by mass, 62–78% of the completed facility is sourced from the property itself: crushed limestone aggregate, dimension sandstone, fluorspar-modified cement, on-site water, and (if grade permits) CaF₂ optical-grade glazing.

On-Site Material Inventory

  • Fluorspar (CaF₂): historical 6,900–7,900 T extracted, host vein widths up to 4 ft mapped. Modern use: cement flux modifier, decorative aggregate, optical glazing feedstock.
  • Limestone host rock: Ste. Genevieve and St. Louis formations — high-quality structural and aggregate stone, locally quarryable.
  • Sandstone (Tar Springs / Cypress / Vienna): dimension-stone quality for cladding, foundation infill.
  • Quartz vein material: decorative and abrasive uses; minor structural.
  • Pennsylvanian shale: brick / ceramic feedstock; secondary aggregate.
  • Aquifer water: on-site well for concrete mixing and process water (subject to IDNR review).

Self-Financing Pathway

The pilot extraction does not need to be a full mining operation. The Indiana Mine alone produced 5,900 T in two years (1923–1925) at hand-tool productivity. A modern 12-month surface and shallow-vein pilot targeting 200–500 T is well within the historical envelope.

Risk Matrix

LOW Aggregate suitability: Ste. Genevieve limestone is a known structural aggregate in southern IL. ASTM C33 testing

Go condition: IDNR pilot-extraction permit issued and the first 50 T extracted assays at ≥85% CaF₂ (met grade or better).

No-go condition: permitting timeline exceeds 18 months or first-50-T grade falls below 70% CaF₂. In that case, defer self-built construction and lease an existing structure in Cave-in-Rock or Rosiclare for the FS-002 compute node, using FS-001 lease/royalty income to cover rent.

Integrated Stack — How The Three Studies Compound

Vertical Integration Logic

FS-001 funds FS-003. Lease bonus and federal grant capture from the owner-operator model produce the working capital for permitting and pilot extraction.

FS-003 builds FS-002. Pilot fluorspar revenue and on-site materials produce the physical shell that houses the compute node.

FS-002 leverages FS-001. The compute node hosts the digital twin and federal mineral-data workloads that justify higher data-license tiers and unlock follow-on federal contracts.

None of the three studies depend on outside equity. The chain is bootstrappable: ISGS core re-analysis ($1–5K) → grant capture ($25–75K) → pilot extraction ($70–250K) → facility shell ($1.6–2.4M) → compute node ($1.8–3.2M). Each stage is self-justifying as a standalone enterprise; together they form a vertically integrated critical-minerals firm operated by a single principal.
